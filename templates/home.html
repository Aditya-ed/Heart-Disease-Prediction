Heart Disease Predictor

This predictor makes use of the data provided by the user to make predictions whether the user has Heart Disease or not

Data Requirments of the Model:

Age:

With increasing age the chances of heart disease increases



Sex:

1 for male and 0 for female



Chest Pain:

Typical Angina, Atypical Angina, Non-Anginal pain and Asymptomatic



Resting Blood Pressure:

Ideal Blood Pressure is considered to be between 90/60mmHg and 120/80mmHg.



Serum Cholesterol:

It represents the amount of total Cholesterol present in their blood



Fasting Blood Sugar:

It indicates to the level of sugar in your blood. The model askes whether the fasting blood sugar level is greater than 120mg/dL



Resting Electrocardiographic Result:

also known as ECG. It takes 3 values:
0: Normal
1: Having ST-T wave abnormality
2: Showing probable or definite left ventricular hypertrophy



Max Heart Rate Achieved:

It represents the Max heart rate achieved during the stress-test



Exercise Induced Angina:

Angina is chest pain caused by reduced blood flow to the heart muscles. Exercise and stress can also cause angina. A person should seek medical attention if it happens.



Oldpeak:

ST depression induced by exercise relative to rest



Slope of ST segment:

Slope of peak exercise ST segment. It has 3 values:
0: Upsloping
1: Flat
2: Downsloping



Fluorosopy:

It gives number of major vessels colored by flourosopy



Thallium Stress Test Result:

It gives information about how well the blood flows through your heart.
1: Fixed Defect(no flow in some part)
2: Normal Flow
3: reversable defect(blood flow detected but it is not normal)
